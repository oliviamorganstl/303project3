About

Human trafficking is a modern-day form of slavery involving the illegal trade of people for exploitation or commercial gain. Traffickers use force, fraud, or coercion to lure their victims and force them into labor or commercial sexual exploitation.


Human trafficking is a hidden crime, as victims rarely come forward to seek help because of language barriers, fear of the traffickers, and/or fear of law enforcement. Utah's goal is to decrease statewide numbers of human trafficking incidents while educating the existing citizens how they can prevent themselves and others from falling prey to these traumatic events.

What does a vulnerable citizen look like?

While all of the human trafficking cases in Utah vary, there are patterns that can be identified from past cases. Utah police would advise individuals to avoid being vulnerable in new situations with unfamiliar individuals. However, because those situations are sometimes difficult to avoid, use this information to self assess the safety of an environment.

  • Ages: 4-32
  • Physical Condition: Injuries or mental illness
  • Location: Impoverished or Homeless
  • Sexual Relations: Intimate partners & LGBTQ+
  • Skills: Unable to speak, submissive, or extremely fearful
  • Behavior: Disconnected from family and friends or no longer attending school
  • Items: Lacking personal possessions and/or legal documents

Human trafficking exists in every country, including the United States. It exists nationwide–in cities, suburbs, and rural towns. You may have heard about sex trafficking, but forced labor is also a significant and prevalent type of human trafficking. Victims are found in legitimate and illegitimate labor industries, including sweatshops, massage parlors, agriculture, restaurants, hotels, and domestic service.


Note that sex trafficking and forced labor are both forms of human trafficking, involving the exploitation of a person. Human trafficking is not the same as smuggling. “Trafficking” is exploitation-based and does not require movement across borders. “Smuggling” is movement-based and involves moving a person across a country’s border with that person’s consent, in violation of immigration laws.
